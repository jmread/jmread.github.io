An Unofficial Guide to INF592

This document is a work in progress.

Glossary

  • Option = INF592 (or INF591 or INF594)

  • Directeur d’Option / INF592 coordinator = Ioana Manolescu and Jesse Read, aka les professeurs responsables du programme de stage INF592

  • Enseignant référent = tuteur = tutor = A professor at DIX, Ecole Polytechnique, assigned to you by Jesse or Ioana (not necessarily = Jesse or Ioana!)

  • Supervisor (at lab or Entreprise where you do the internship)

  • SOIE = Stages, Orientation, Insertion et relation Entreprises

  • Track = filière; https://www.enseignement.polytechnique.fr/informatique/3A/

1.A Getting Started When you Already have a Contact/Internship-Supervisor in Mind

Get them to write an up a topic Description for the Internship with the following information:

  • Title

  • Topic

  • City and country

  • Team or project in the lab

  • Name and mail of the advisor

  • Name and mail of the head of the department

  • General presentation of the topic (roughly 5 to 10 lines)

  • Objective of the internship (roughly 10 to 20 lines)

  • Bibliographic references

  • Expected ability of the student

Most importantly, the it should describe: a research goal, a problem, and a concrete starting point on how to solve it.

Does not need to provide a salary. There is no obligation that you are paid during the internship, although, of course, it would be nice if this was the case.

Note that we will not validate topics that do not contain the above information and do not appear to be suitable research internships.

Your supervisor will have to provide a report at the end of the internship, and hopefuly attend your defense. We consider their input when giving you a grade for your internship.

Think ahead, to validate INF592, you need to bear in mind:

  • You need a research internship.

  • You need results to be evaluated by a jury at X (it means that it’s a problem for you if the company refuses to let you present them due to sensitivity issues / requires an overly restrictive NDA that X does not want to sign)

1.B Getting Started When you do not have a Contact/Internship Supervisor in mind

Choose an internship from the list and contact the potential supervisor, or find a potential supervisor and ask them to submit an internship topic.

Check that your internship is OK. We try to filter out (not approve) interships, but we do so based on the internship topic, we cannot know the full intentions/conditions not expressed — so make sure to ask your potential supervisor about this.

If in doubt, look for another topic/host.

2. Double Check: Is your internship OK? Are you INF591 or INF592 or INF593 ?

Is your internship OK, according to the criteria described above?

If you are in INF591 or INF593 but your topic is listed, or would be better listed under INF592, you can ask scolarité (with Ioana and Jesse in copy) to change. If OK, we will reply something like "C’est OK pour nous pour INF592", again with scolarité in copy.

3. Getting an "Enseignant Référent"

Checklist:

  • Your topic has been validated by Ioana or Jesse

  • You have the agreement of your internship supervisor to proceed

  • You have chosen/agreed on compatible starting (earliest: end of March) and ending dates (latest: end of August — it may go longer if you wish as far as the company/lab is concerned, but insofar as INF592 is concerned, it must be ended before September)

Now you contact Ioana and Jesse to get the final OK and an Enseignant Référent assigned to your internship (if you don’t already have one). Generally, a DIX professors (full-time and part-time) share the "référent" work, each taking on a number of internships (usually 3-5 per person, maximum 16).

4. The "Fiche de Stage"

Now you fill in the Fiche de Stage form, carefully check all the details of the form (supervisor name, enseignant référant (not the same as supervisor), details, dates, course code, topic, …) and send it to Ioana and Jesse for signing. We will send a copy to the DIX Assistants — they need to confirm who is assigned to who (especially, the enseignant référant).

5. The "Convention de Stage"

Send the signed Fiche de Stage to SOIE, and they will create the Convention de Stage for you. (The DIX Assistants may forward it on to SOIE for you — in which case you will be in copy of the email). The SOIE will take care of obtaining all relevant signatures via LiveConsent.
