I am a Professor (HDR) in the DaSciM team of the Data Analytics and Machine Learning pôle of the Computer Science Laboratory (LIX) at École Polytechnique, Institut Polytechnique de Paris.

My main research interests involves multi-target learning, probabilistic inference, and learning from dynamic and evolving data streams, but I am open to much more, including applications in medicine and energy.

Prior to becoming Professor (HDR 2017) I was Assistant Professor (also at LIX, Ecole Polytechnique), following postdoc research at INFRES Télécom Paris (2016), Aalto University, Finland (2013-2016) and the Dept. TSC at the Universidad Carlos III de Madrid, Spain (2010-2013). I obtained my PhD in 2010 from the University of Waikato, New Zealand. I obtained a Bachelor of Computing and Mathematical Sciences (Hons.) also at Waikato.
